An app to modernize the 311 service request system.
Informatics Capstone Project

The 311 Service Request system is a powerful, but rarely used, goverment service (similar to 911 or 411) that users can call to report civic issues such as graffiti, broken glass, or downed powerlines.
Over the course of two quarters, I and a partner designed a fully functional iPhone/Android application that leverages the capabilities of modern smartphones to make reports quicker and easier.


Notably the application uses computer vision to automatically fill out much of the information necessary for a report. This new design is not only easier and faster than describing issues over the phone, it provides more useful information to the government.

User-crafted reports are structured into a JSON document and pushed to a Firebase DB, where they can be accessed and queried from later.

While the project is no longer in active development,
all project files and resources are available on GitHub, check it out!
