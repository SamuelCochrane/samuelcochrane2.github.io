Database

Fully functional relational database design for a fictional supermarket. Includes multiple Stored Procedures for easy writing of information. Built using SQLServer and Visio.
Final project for INFO 343 - Advanced Database Design.

One fine body…

As a final project for INFO 343, Advanced Database Design, I was tasked with building a fully functional and complex database.
The database was built to fulfil the needs of a fictional supermarket. The database as such tracks products (including nutritional facts, prices, and stock), as well as user carts, allowing the store to track the purchases of its customers on both a per-item and per-trip basis.
To kick things up a notch, I also included functionality for the users to track recipies, rate & comment on products, and list their allergies (so that they can avoid buying products with certain ingredients).

This database is fully normalized to the third normal form, with no data redundancy or possibility for 'infinite loops'.
The final submission included several Stored Procecures for insertion of complex data (such as adding a new product with ingredients, nutritional facts, categorization, etc.).
The DB also includes several business rules that automatically send out alerts after certain actions are flagged, such as low/empty stock on items, or a user adding an item to their cart that they are allergic to.
